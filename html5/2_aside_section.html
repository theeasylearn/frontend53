Gujarati Recipe

Gujarati Kadhi

Ingredients

  1. 2 cups buttermilk (chaas)
  2. 2 tbsp gram flour (besan)
  3. 1 tsp ginger-green chilli paste
  4. 1 tsp sugar
  5. Salt to taste
  6. For tempering: 1 tsp ghee, jeera, curry leaves, hing, dry red chilli

Preparation Method

Whisk besan into buttermilk until smooth. Add ginger-chilli paste, sugar and salt. Cook on medium flame stirring continuously until it boils. For tempering, heat ghee, add cumin seeds, curry leaves, hing and dry red chilli. Pour this tempering over kadhi. Serve hot with steamed rice or roti. This comforting sour-sweet kadhi is a staple in Gujarati dinner thali.

Dal Dhokli

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup toor dal (arhar dal)
  2. 1 cup whole wheat flour (for dhokli)
  3. 1 tsp turmeric powder
  4. 1 tsp red chilli powder
  5. Jaggery and kokum for sweet-sour taste
  6. Whole spices (clove, cinnamon, mustard seeds)
  7. Peanuts and curry leaves

Preparation Method

Cook toor dal in pressure cooker with turmeric and salt. Knead wheat flour dough with spices and roll into thin rotis, cut into diamond shapes (dhokli). Add dhokli pieces into boiling dal along with jaggery, kokum, peanuts and tempering of mustard, cumin, cloves and curry leaves. Simmer for 10-12 minutes until dhokli is cooked. Serve hot with ghee on top. A complete one-pot Gujarati dinner dish.

Undhiyu

Ingredients

  1. 250g surti papdi, 250g yam, 250g sweet potato
  2. 4-5 small brinjals
  3. 1 cup fresh methi leaves
  4. 1 cup grated coconut
  5. Green garlic, coriander, green chilli
  6. Spices: turmeric, coriander powder, garam masala

Preparation Method

Prepare methi muthiya and shallow fry. Mix coconut, coriander, green garlic and spices for stuffing. Stuff brinjals and mix all vegetables with stuffing masala. In a heavy bottomed pan or pressure cooker, layer vegetables with oil and cook on low flame for 30-40 minutes until all vegetables are tender. Stir gently occasionally. Famous winter Gujarati mixed vegetable dish best served with puri or roti.

Handvo

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup rice
  2. 1/2 cup chana dal, 1/2 cup toor dal, 1/4 cup urad dal
  3. 1 cup bottle gourd (dudhi) grated
  4. 1/2 cup yogurt
  5. Green chilli, ginger, turmeric
  6. Mustard seeds, sesame seeds for tempering

Preparation Method

Soak rice and dals for 4-5 hours and grind into coarse batter. Add grated bottle gourd, yogurt, turmeric, ginger-chilli paste and salt. Let it ferment overnight. Pour batter into greased baking tray or handvo pan. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top. Cook on stove or bake at 180°C for 35-40 minutes until golden and crisp. A healthy savory Gujarati dinner cake.

Sev Tameta nu Shaak

Ingredients

  1. 4-5 ripe tomatoes
  2. 1 cup nylon sev
  3. 1 tsp mustard seeds
  4. 1 tsp cumin seeds
  5. 1 tsp red chilli powder
  6. 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  7. Sugar and salt to taste
  8. Coriander for garnishing

Preparation Method

Heat oil in a pan, add mustard and cumin seeds. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chilli powder, salt and sugar. Cook until tomatoes become soft and mushy. Add little water if needed. Just before serving, mix in nylon sev. Garnish with chopped coriander. Serve immediately with roti, puri or bajra rotla. A quick and popular Gujarati dinner sabzi.

Butter Chicken

Ingredients

  1. 500g boneless chicken
  2. 1 cup fresh cream
  3. 2 tbsp butter
  4. 1 cup tomato puree
  5. 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  6. Spices: garam masala, red chilli powder, kasuri methi
  7. Yogurt and lemon for marination

Preparation Method

Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chilli powder and salt for 2 hours. Grill or shallow fry the chicken pieces. In a pan, heat butter, sauté ginger-garlic, add tomato puree and spices. Cook until oil separates. Add grilled chicken and cream. Simmer for 10 minutes. Finish with kasuri methi and more butter. Serve hot with naan or jeera rice.

Dal Makhani

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup whole black urad dal
  2. 1/2 cup rajma (kidney beans)
  3. 1 cup fresh cream
  4. 3 tbsp butter
  5. 2 tbsp tomato puree
  6. Ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, red chilli powder
  7. Kasuri methi for garnish

Preparation Method

Soak urad dal and rajma overnight. Pressure cook until very soft. Mash the dal lightly. In a heavy pan, heat butter, sauté ginger-garlic paste, add tomato puree and spices. Cook till masala leaves oil. Add cooked dal, salt and water. Simmer on low flame for 30-40 minutes. Add cream and more butter. Garnish with kasuri methi. Best served with naan or roti.

Sarson da Saag

Ingredients

  1. 500g mustard leaves (sarson)
  2. 200g spinach leaves
  3. 2-3 green chillies
  4. 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  5. 2 tbsp maize flour (makki atta)
  6. Ghee, onion, tomato for tadka
  7. White butter for serving

Preparation Method

Wash and chop mustard and spinach leaves. Pressure cook with green chillies, ginger and salt. Blend into a coarse puree. Heat ghee, sauté ginger-garlic, onion and tomato. Add the saag puree and maize flour. Cook for 15-20 minutes on low flame. Finish with a tadka of ghee, garlic and red chilli. Serve with makki di roti and white butter.

Punjabi Chole Masala

Ingredients

  1. 2 cups chickpeas (chole)
  2. 2 large onions
  3. 3 tomatoes
  4. 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  5. Chole masala, garam masala, red chilli powder
  6. Tea bag for dark color
  7. Coriander and green chilli for garnish

Preparation Method

Soak chickpeas overnight and boil with salt and tea bag. Heat oil, fry onions till golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes and all spices. Cook until masala is well roasted. Add boiled chickpeas and simmer for 15 minutes. Mash few chickpeas for thickness. Garnish with coriander and green chilli. Serve with bhature, puri or jeera rice.

Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients

  1. 250g paneer cubes
  2. 3-4 tomatoes
  3. 1 large onion
  4. 1 cup fresh cream
  5. 2 tbsp butter
  6. Ginger-garlic paste, kasuri methi
  7. Red chilli powder, garam masala, sugar

Preparation Method

Blanch tomatoes and make smooth puree. Heat butter, sauté onion, ginger-garlic paste. Add tomato puree, red chilli powder and cook till oil separates. Add cream, kasuri methi, sugar and garam masala. Add paneer cubes and simmer for 5-7 minutes. Serve hot with naan, roti or jeera rice. A rich and creamy vegetarian Punjabi favorite.
